By [[Akihiro Nishi]], [[Hirokazu Shirado]], [[Nicholas Christakis]]

Terminology

[[Erdos Renyi Graph]], [[Gini Coefficient]], [[Social Comparison]], [[homophily]],[[end-game effects]] [[Ordinary Least Squares]]

Summary

In this experiment, agents are assigned to nodes in an [[Erdos Renyi Graph]] G(n,n(n−1)10)G(n,\frac{n(n-1)}{10})G(n,10n(n−1)​), which is rewired with rate rrr. When an edge is subject to rewiring, one of the nodal agents is allowed to unilaterally decide to either create the edge (if it does not exist), destroy the edge (if it does), or do nothing; rnrnrn edges are subject to rewiring every time step. Agents can choose costly cooperation or costless defection.

An intermediate rrr induces high levels of cooperation, thus increasing average wealth and decreasing the gini coefficient (C-C benefits both ends of the dyad by the same amount). Intuitively, this is because when edges are rarely subject to reevaluation, the threat of punishment via edge destruction is not credible, and when they are constantly subject, the promise of long-term collaboration is not credible.
